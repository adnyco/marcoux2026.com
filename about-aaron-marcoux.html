Commitment to Public Service

Aaron Marcoux’s legal career reflects sustained preparation for the responsibilities of judicial service. Through public service across Wisconsin’s court system, he has gained experience from multiple perspectives while maintaining a consistent focus on professionalism, impartiality, and the careful administration of justice.

A lifelong Wisconsin resident, Aaron lives in Shell Lake, Wisconsin, and has served Washburn County in various legal roles since 2019.

Experience Across Wisconsin’s Courts

Washburn County District Attorney (2019–Present)

In September 2019, following a vacancy in the office, Governor Tony Evers appointed Aaron Marcoux as Washburn County District Attorney. He subsequently stood for election, was elected to a full term in 2020, and re-elected in 2024.

As District Attorney, Marcoux is responsible for prosecuting criminal matters in the county and managing a full courtroom calendar. His work emphasizes professional preparation, orderly case management, and timely court proceedings, while maintaining respect for the role of the court and the rights of all parties involved.

Assistant District Attorney — Washburn County (2019)

Prior to his appointment as District Attorney, Marcoux served as Washburn County’s first full-time Assistant District Attorney, a newly created position that expanded the county’s prosecution capacity. This role provided additional experience working within Washburn County’s courts immediately prior to his appointment to office.

Assistant District Attorney — Sawyer County (2015–2019)

Marcoux served four years as an Assistant District Attorney in Sawyer County, handling a broad range of criminal matters. This role strengthened his courtroom experience and further developed his understanding of case preparation, evidentiary standards, and professional practice within the court system.

Wisconsin Assistant State Public Defender (2010–2015)

Marcoux began his legal career in public defense, serving in the Ashland office and representing clients across Ashland, Bayfield, and Iron counties. This foundational work provided firsthand experience on the defense side of the justice system and reinforced the importance of constitutional protections, thoughtful advocacy, and careful preparation.

Community & Professional Recognition

Aaron Marcoux’s contributions have been recognized by local and statewide institutions, both for his legal service and his leadership in the community.

2024 — UW–Eau Claire–Barron County Distinguished Alumnus

Marcoux was honored by his alma mater as the 2024 Distinguished Alumnus, recognizing his public service and professional integrity as Washburn County’s District Attorney.

2021 — WEDC “Success Story” for Community Leadership

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Marcoux helped lead Community First Washburn County, coordinating emergency food distribution and support services for local residents. The Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation featured this effort as a statewide model of rural response.

2019 — Appointment as District Attorney

Governor Tony Evers appointed Marcoux to fill a vacancy as Washburn County District Attorney, citing his local ties, integrity, and practical ideas for strengthening the justice system.

2013 — Wisconsin Court System Mention

As an Assistant State Public Defender, Marcoux was part of a county team selected for the state’s Evidence-Based Decision Making initiative. His participation was noted in the Wisconsin court system’s newsletter, The Third Branch.

2026 Nonpartisan Judicial Election

Aaron Marcoux is a candidate for Washburn County Circuit Court Judge in the nonpartisan Spring Election on April 7, 2026. His candidacy reflects broad experience across Wisconsin’s justice system and a continued commitment to the responsibilities of judicial service, including fairness, efficiency, and respect for every person who comes before the court.
