Experience in Wisconsin’s Courts

Throughout his career, Aaron Marcoux has pursued public service across the justice system — gaining experience from multiple perspectives and remaining committed to the responsibilities and impact of the judicial role.

His work reflects a continued dedication to contributing to and strengthening Wisconsin’s legal system through professionalism, preparation, and care in the administration of justice.

Washburn County District Attorney (2019–Present)

In September 2019, following the resignation of the prior district attorney, Governor Tony Evers appointed Aaron Marcoux as Washburn County District Attorney. He subsequently stood for election, was elected to the office in 2020, and re-elected in 2024.

As District Attorney, Marcoux is responsible for overseeing and prosecuting the county’s criminal caseload. His work emphasizes professional preparation, efficient case management, and timely court proceedings, while maintaining respect for the rights of all parties and the role of the court.

Assistant District Attorney — Washburn County (2019)

In August 2019, Marcoux joined the Washburn County District Attorney’s Office as the county’s first full-time Assistant District Attorney, a newly created position. Prior to this, the county had operated with only a part-time ADA.

His time in this role coincided with leadership transitions within the office and provided additional experience managing criminal matters in Washburn County.

Assistant District Attorney — Sawyer County (2015–2019)

Marcoux served as an Assistant District Attorney in Sawyer County for four years, handling a broad criminal caseload. His work included a significant focus on drug-related and public safety cases, providing extensive courtroom experience and further developing his understanding of case preparation, evidentiary standards, and trial practice.

Wisconsin Assistant State Public Defender (2010–2015)

Aaron Marcoux began his legal career in public service as a Wisconsin Assistant State Public Defender in late 2010. Based in the Ashland office, he represented clients across Ashland, Bayfield, and Iron counties in northern Wisconsin.

Over five years in this role, he gained firsthand experience on the defense side of the justice system and developed a practical understanding of courtroom procedure, constitutional protections, and the importance of thoughtful, even-tempered advocacy.

Recognition & Public Service

In 2024, Marcoux was named Distinguished Alumnus by the UW–Eau Claire–Barron County Foundation. A graduate of UW–Eau Claire–Barron County (Class of 2001), he received the honor at the May 2024 commencement ceremony in recognition of his public service and contributions to the community.

Judicial Candidacy (2026)

With more than a decade of experience serving Wisconsin’s public courts — as a public defender, prosecutor, and elected district attorney — Aaron Marcoux is a candidate for Washburn County Circuit Court Judge in the April 7, 2026 nonpartisan election. His background reflects broad experience across the justice system and a long-standing commitment to public service.
